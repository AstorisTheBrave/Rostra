## what this is

Plays music in voice channels: members queue songs by search or URL and control playback. Audio is handled
by external **Lavalink** nodes (configured with failover); the bot just sends commands to them.

## technical map

**file paths**
- command: `src/modules/music/index.ts` (`/music`)
- Lavalink manager + events: `src/modules/music/service.ts`, `src/modules/music/events.ts`
- nodes: `config.lavalink.nodes` (from `LAVALINK_NODES` env)

**command** - `/music play <query> | skip | stop | pause | resume | queue | nowplaying | volume <percent>
| loop <off|track|queue>`.

**library** - `lavalink-client` v2 (Lavalink v4). `getManager(client)` builds a `LavalinkManager` from the
configured nodes; `initMusic(client)` runs on `ready` (node connect, `trackStart`/`queueEnd` announcements)
and registers the untyped `"raw"` gateway listener so voice state reaches Lavalink.

## gotchas

- **Needs a running Lavalink node** to actually play - node creds come from `LAVALINK_NODES` (10 public
  nodes are in the local `.env`). Multi-node failover + auto-reconnect are configured.
- The `"raw"` client event isn't in discord.js's typed `ClientEvents`, so it's registered directly in
  `initMusic` (localized cast) rather than via `defineEvent`.
- The manager is built lazily and only when nodes are configured; `/music` reports "unavailable" otherwise.
- **Not yet smoke-tested against a live node** - typechecks, lints, and loads; verify playback on a real
  Lavalink connection. discord.js `GuildVoiceStates` intent is enabled.
- No database; player/queue state lives in Lavalink + the client process.
